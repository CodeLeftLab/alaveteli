Which of these is happening?

  • The authority say that they need a postal address, not just an email, for it to be a valid FOI request

    The law, the Ministry of Justice and the Information Commissioner all say that an email is sufficient (more details).

    At the bottom of this page, write a reply to the authority explaining this to them.

  • The authority only has a paper copy of the information.

    At the bottom of this page, write a reply to them trying to persuade them to scan it in (more details).

  • You want to give your postal address to the authority in private.

    To do that please send a private email to Government Equalities Office <equalities@localhost> containing your postal address, and asking them to reply to this request. Or you could phone them.

    When you receive the paper response, please help others find out what it says:

    • Add an annotation to your request with choice quotes, or a summary of the response.
    • If you can, scan in or photograph the response, and send us a copy to upload.

Send a public follow up message to the main FOI contact at Government Equalities Office

Advice:

  • Please only write messages directly relating to your request Civil partnerships for opposite sex couples costs.
  • If you would like to ask for information that was not in your original request, then file a new request.

The response to your request is long overdue. You can say that, by law, under all circumstances, the authority should have responded by now (details).
